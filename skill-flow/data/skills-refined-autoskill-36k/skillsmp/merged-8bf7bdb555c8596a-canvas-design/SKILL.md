# Canvas Design

Create visually striking static designs using design philosophies that are expressed through form, space, color, and composition. This skill involves two main steps: creating a design philosophy and then expressing it visually.

## Step 1: Design Philosophy Creation

To begin, create a **Visual Philosophy** that will be interpreted through:
- Form, space, color, composition
- Images, graphics, shapes, patterns
- Minimal text as visual accent

### The Critical Understanding
- **Input**: Subtle instructions from the user that guide the creative process without constraining it.
- **Output**: A design philosophy/aesthetic movement.
- **Next Step**: The philosophy will be visually expressed, creating artifacts that are 90% visual design and 10% essential text.

### How to Generate a Visual Philosophy

1. **Name the Movement** (1-2 words): Examples include "Brutalist Joy," "Chromatic Silence," or "Metabolist Dreams."
2. **Articulate the Philosophy** (4-6 paragraphs):
   - Express how the philosophy manifests through space, form, color, material, scale, rhythm, composition, and visual hierarchy.
   - Emphasize craftsmanship, ensuring the final work appears meticulously crafted and the product of deep expertise.

### Critical Guidelines
- Avoid redundancy; each design aspect should be mentioned once.
- Emphasize craftsmanship repeatedly, using phrases like "meticulously crafted" and "painstaking attention."
- Leave creative space for interpretation while being specific about the aesthetic direction.

### Essential Principles
- **Visual Philosophy**: Create an aesthetic worldview to be expressed through design.
- **Minimal Text**: Text should be sparse and essential, integrated as a visual element.
- **Spatial Expression**: Ideas communicate through space, form, color, and composition.
- **Artistic Freedom**: Allow for interpretive choices at a high level of craftsmanship.
- **Pure Design**: Focus on creating art objects, not merely decorative documents.
- **Expert Craftsmanship**: The final work must look labored over with care, showcasing expertise.

## Step 2: Canvas Creation

With the philosophy established, express it on a canvas. Use the design philosophy as the foundation to create a single-page, highly visual, design-forward PDF or PNG output.

### Important Considerations
- Use repeating patterns and perfect shapes, treating the design as a scientific observation.
- Add sparse, clinical typography that suggests a diagram from an imaginary discipline.
- Ensure that text is minimal and visual-first, with proper margins and breathing room for all elements.

### Achieving Human-Crafted Quality
- Create work that looks like it took countless hours, ensuring every detail is perfect.
- Refine the existing composition to enhance cohesiveness and artistic integrity.

### Multi-Page Option
When requested, create additional pages that are unique twists on the original design, telling a story in a tasteful way.
