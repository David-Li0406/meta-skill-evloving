# Template: Nurture Email Sequence

## Purpose
Build relationship with new subscribers over time. Establish trust before asking for anything.

## Best rhetoric devices
- **Anadiplosis** for narrative flow
- **Litotes** for humble, relatable tone
- **Epistrophe** for memorable closes

## Sequence Overview (7 emails over 3 weeks)

| Day | Email | Purpose | CTA |
|-----|-------|---------|-----|
| 0 | Welcome | Set expectations, deliver lead magnet | None |
| 2 | Quick win | Immediate value, build credibility | Reply |
| 5 | Story | Personal connection, relatability | None |
| 8 | Deep value | Substantial teaching | Free resource |
| 12 | Philosophy | Your worldview, differentiation | None |
| 16 | Soft pitch | Introduce paid offering | Learn more |
| 21 | Transition | Move to regular newsletter | Choose frequency |

---

## Email 1: Welcome (Day 0)

```
Subject: Your [lead magnet] is ready

Here it is: [LINK]

I'm [Your name]. I [what you do and for whom].

Quick background: [1-2 sentences about your experience
and what led you to do this].

What you can expect from these emails:

- [Promise 1 - what kind of content]
- [Promise 2 - your unique angle]
- [Promise 3 - format/frequency]

I send roughly one email per week. You can unsubscribe
anytime - no hard feelings.

For now, enjoy the [lead magnet]. If you have questions,
hit reply. I read every response.

[Your name]
```

---

## Email 2: Quick Win (Day 2)

```
Subject: The first thing I [do/build/check] in every [project]

Before I [tackle anything complex], I [do this one thing]:

[Simple, actionable advice]

Every time. No matter how complex the final [outcome] will be.

Here's why:

If this simple case doesn't work, nothing will.

[Example - code, process, or demonstration]

That's it. [Brevity statement].

From here, I add complexity piece by piece. Each addition
either works or breaks something. When it breaks, I know
exactly what caused it.

The [people] who struggle most are the ones who try to
[do everything at once].

Start simple. Verify as you go.

Does this match how you approach [relevant activity]?
Hit reply - I'm curious about your process.

[Your name]
```

---

## Email 3: Story (Day 5)

```
Subject: The [project/thing] that almost [dramatic consequence]

True story.

Early in my career, I [situation setup].

[Context about the task/goal].

I [did the work]. [Delivered/completed] the [output].
[Expected positive outcome].

The [person/client] [unexpected negative reaction].

[Elaborate on why - the real issue]

They asked me to "[the unreasonable request]."

I understood what they meant: [the subtext].

I didn't. The [work] was right.

What I did wrong was [the actual mistake - usually about
communication, expectation setting, or understanding needs].

The lesson that stuck with me:

[One clear lesson in a memorable sentence]

Now I start every [project/engagement] with: "[The better
question/approach]"

Better to [preventive action] before you [start work].

[Your name]

P.S. [Brief resolution to the story - what happened eventually]
```

---

## Email 4: Deep Value (Day 8)

```
Subject: The [approach/pattern/method] that changed everything for me

There's a [pattern/technique/approach] I use in almost every [project] now.

It's not in most tutorials. I figured it out after years
of hitting the same problems.

I call it the "[Name]" [pattern/approach/method].

The problem: [Problem description - something your audience
faces regularly]

The solution: [Your approach explained]

[Detailed explanation with example - code, process, or framework]

Now in your [work]:

[Show how to apply it]

After you [complete the work]:

[Show the benefit/result]

This single [pattern/approach] made my [work] [specific improvement].

I wrote a full guide on this with more examples:
[LINK TO FREE RESOURCE]

[Your name]
```

## Email 5: Philosophy (Day 12)

```
Subject: Why I [your approach] (not [common alternative])

People sometimes ask why I focus on [your method/approach].

[Alternatives] have [their advantages]. [Acknowledge what
they offer honestly].

Here's my honest answer:

I've [experienced those alternatives]. [Describe your
experience with them].

And I've watched [people] become [negative consequence].

[Your approach] is different.

[Benefit 1]. [Benefit 2]. [Benefit 3].

You [positive outcomes]. You [empowerment]. You [control].

Is it harder to learn? [Honest assessment]. But you're
learning [transferable skill].

Is [common objection]? [Your reframe].

Does it have [limitation]? [Your honest answer and workaround].

I [do what I do] because I believe [your core philosophy].

That might not be the right choice for everyone.
But it's why I'm here.

[Your name]
```

---

## Email 6: Soft Pitch (Day 16)

```
Subject: If you want to go further

For the past two weeks, I've been sharing [topic] tips
and stories.

If you've found them useful, there's more where that
came from.

The [Your Offering Name] is my complete [format] for
[target audience] who want to [desired outcome].

It's structured learning. Real projects. Direct support.

Not for everyone - it requires [honest requirement].

But if you're serious about [becoming/achieving something],
it's the fastest path I know.

You can see what's inside here: [LINK]

No pressure. These emails will keep coming either way.

But I wanted you to know it exists.

[Your name]
```

---

## Email 7: Transition (Day 21)

```
Subject: What happens next

You've been getting these "welcome" emails for three weeks.

Starting now, you'll get my regular newsletter instead.

Same voice. Same mix of [content types].
Just less frequent - usually once a week.

If that's too much, you can switch to monthly updates
or unsubscribe entirely: [LINK]

No hard feelings either way. Your inbox, your rules.

Thanks for reading this far.

[Your name]

P.S. If you ever have a [topic] question, just reply.
I read every response.
```

---

## Voice notes for nurture sequences
- Build trust slowly - no selling until email 6
- More personal, less polished than other content
- Stories > tips in early emails
- Ask for replies, not clicks
- Acknowledge that you're in their inbox by invitation
