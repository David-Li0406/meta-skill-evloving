# Competitive Analysis

## Overview

Competitive analysis systematically evaluates competitors to understand market positioning, identify gaps, and inform strategic decisions. This skill provides comprehensive frameworks and methodologies for thorough competitive intelligence gathering.

## When to Use

- Entering a new market or segment
- Evaluating product positioning
- Identifying feature gaps
- Understanding pricing dynamics
- Assessing competitive threats
- Planning differentiation strategy

## Core Frameworks

### Porter's Five Forces

Analyze industry structure and competitive intensity through five forces:

1. **Threat of New Entrants**
   - Barriers to entry: capital requirements, economies of scale, brand loyalty, etc.
   - **Indicator**: High threat = low barriers, easy to enter.

2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers**
   - Factors: supplier concentration, switching costs, unique inputs.
   - **Indicator**: High power = cost pressure.

3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers**
   - Factors: buyer concentration, price sensitivity, product differentiation.
   - **Indicator**: High power = pricing pressure.

4. **Threat of Substitutes**
   - Consider alternatives and price-performance trade-offs.
   - **Indicator**: High threat = innovation pressure.

5. **Competitive Rivalry**
   - Factors: number of competitors, industry growth rate, product differentiation.
   - **Indicator**: High rivalry = challenging margins.

## Research Process

1. **Identify Competitors**
   - Direct, indirect, potential entrants, and substitutes.

2. **Gather Intelligence**
   - Use public sources like company websites, press releases, financial reports, and customer reviews.

3. **Analyze Findings**
   - Map to Porter's Five Forces, build competitor matrix, and create positioning map.

4. **Synthesize Insights**
   - Identify competitive advantages, market gaps, and strategic recommendations.

## Best Practices

- Update analysis quarterly at minimum.
- Cross-validate from multiple sources.
- Distinguish facts from speculation.
- Track competitor job postings for strategy hints.

## Quick Start

To analyze the competitive landscape:

1. Identify competitors - direct, indirect, and future threats.
2. Apply Porter's Five Forces - assess industry attractiveness.
3. Create a positioning map - visualize competitive space.
4. Profile top 3-5 competitors - deep dive on key rivals.
5. Identify differentiation - what makes you unique.
6. Analyze pricing - where do you fit?
7. Assess advantages - what's defensible?
8. Develop strategy - how to win.
