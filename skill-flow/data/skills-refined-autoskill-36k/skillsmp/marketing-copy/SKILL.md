# Marketing Copy Skill

Proven copywriting frameworks and best practices for creating compelling marketing content across different channels.

## Core Copywriting Principles

1. **Know Your Audience**: Write for a specific person, not "everyone"
2. **Focus on Benefits**: Features tell, benefits sell
3. **One Clear Message**: Don't confuse with multiple ideas
4. **Create Urgency**: Give reason to act now
5. **Overcome Objections**: Address concerns preemptively
6. **Social Proof**: Use testimonials and numbers
7. **Clear CTA**: Tell them exactly what to do next

## Classic Copywriting Frameworks

### AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

```
Attention: Grab attention with bold headline
"Stop Wasting Hours on Manual Data Entry"

Interest: Build interest with relatable problem
"Every day, your team spends 3+ hours copying data between systems. 
That's 15 hours per week—nearly half a workweek—on mindless tasks."

Desire: Create desire by showing the solution
"Imagine if all that data synced automatically. Your team could focus 
on what actually matters: growing your business."

Action: Clear call to action
"Start your free 14-day trial—no credit card required."
```

### PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solve)

```
Problem: Identify the problem
"Managing multiple social media accounts is overwhelming."

Agitate: Make the problem feel worse
"You're juggling 5 different apps, missing engagement opportunities, 
and your posts are inconsistent. Meanwhile, competitors are gaining 
followers while you're stuck in scheduling hell."

Solve: Present your solution
"PostPilot combines all your social accounts in one dashboard. Schedule 
a month of content in 30 minutes."
```

### FAB (Features, Advantages, Benefits)

```
Feature: "AI-powered email campaigns"
Advantage: "Writes subject lines that get 40% more opens"
Benefit: "Reach more customers without hiring a copywriter"

Feature: "Real-time analytics dashboard"
Advantage: "See which campaigns perform best instantly"
Benefit: "Make data-driven decisions that grow revenue"
```

### The 4 Ps (Promise, Picture, Proof, Push)

```
Promise: "Get your first paying customer in 30 days"

Picture: "Imagine waking up to notification: 'You have a new customer!' 
Your product is solving real problems. People are actually paying for it."

Proof: "Join 10,000+ founders who launched with our framework. 
Average time to first sale: 23 days."

Push: "Start today and get your personal launch checklist free."
```

## Channel-Specific Copy

### Landing Page Headline Formulas

```typescript
// Number + Adjective + Target + Promise
"7 Simple Steps to Double Your Email List"

// How to + Desire + Timeframe
"How to Build a SaaS Business in 90 Days"

// Achieve [Benefit] Without [Pain Point]
"Get More Leads Without Spending on Ads"

// [Do Something] Like [World Class Example]
"Write Copy Like a $500/Hour Copywriter"

// What/Why/How + Target Audience
"Why Enterprise Teams Choose Our Platform"
```

### Value Proposition Framework

```
[Product Name] helps [Target Audience] [Achieve Benefit] by [Key Differentiator]

Examples:
"Notion helps remote teams stay organized by combining docs, tasks, and 
wikis in one workspace."

"Superhuman helps busy executives reach inbox zero by making email 
blazingly fast with keyboard shortcuts."

"Stripe helps developers accept payments by providing APIs that handle 
the complexity of global payments."
```

## Product Page Copy Structure

```markdown
# Above the Fold
## Headline: Clear value proposition in 5-10 words
"Email marketing that grows with your business"

## Subheadline: Expand on the promise (10-20 words)
"Send beautiful campaigns, automate your workflow, and turn 
subscribers into customers—all in one platform."

## CTA: Primary action
[Start Free Trial]

## Social Proof: Quick credibility
"Trusted by 50,000+ businesses"

# Features Section
## Benefit-First Feature Headlines

### ❌ Feature-focused:
"Advanced Segmentation Engine"

### ✅ Benefit-focused:
"Send the Right Message to the Right People"
Automatically group subscribers by behavior, so every email feels personal.

### ❌ Feature-focused:
"A/B Testing Dashboard"

### ✅ Benefit-focused:
"Know What Works Before You Send"
Test subject lines, content, and send times to maximize opens and clicks.

# Social Proof Section
## Testimonials
"[Product] helped us increase signups by 300% in just 2 months."
— Sarah Johnson, VP Marketing @ TechCorp

## Numbers that Matter
- 10M+ emails sent daily
- 45% average open rate
- 4.8/5 star rating from 2,000+ reviews

# Pricing Section
## Frame Pricing Around Value

❌ "Professional Plan: $99/month"
✅ "Everything you need to grow: $99/month"

❌ List of features
✅ "Perfect for teams sending 50,000+ emails monthly"

# Final CTA
## Overcome Last Objections

"Start your free 14-day trial. No credit card required. 
Cancel anytime."
```

## Social Media Copy

### Twitter/X

```typescript
// Hook patterns
"Here's what nobody tells you about [topic]:"
"I spent $50k learning [lesson]. Here's what I discovered:"
"This [number]-step process helped me [achieve result]:"

// Thread structure
1. Hook (grab attention)
2. Context (why it matters)
3. Steps/Points (value content)
4. Recap (reinforce message)
5. CTA (what to do next)

Example:
"I analyzed 1,000 SaaS landing pages. Here's what converts: 🧵

1. Clear headline (5-10 words)
2. One primary CTA above fold
3. Social proof within first screen
4. Benefits > Features
5. Risk reversal (guarantee/free trial)

Want the full breakdown? 👇"
```

### LinkedIn

```typescript
// Professional storytelling
Opening: Personal story or surprising stat
"I lost $10,000 on my first product launch."

Body: The journey/learning
"Here's what I learned: customers don't buy features, 
they buy outcomes. I was selling 'AI-powered analytics' 
when I should've been selling 'know which customers 
will churn before they do.'"

Conclusion: Key takeaway
"The lesson? Talk about the destination, not the car."

CTA: Engagement question
"What's the best marketing lesson you've learned the hard way?"
```

## Common Copywriting Mistakes

```typescript
// ❌ Being clever instead of clear
"We make your data dance"
// ✅ Being clear
"Turn your data into actionable insights"

// ❌ Talking about yourself
"We're the leading provider of..."
// ✅ Talking about customer benefits
"You'll get faster insights to grow your business"

// ❌ Using jargon
"Leverage our synergistic platform to optimize your workflow"
// ✅ Using plain language
"Work faster with tools that actually work together"

// ❌ Weak or passive voice
"Results can be achieved"
// ✅ Active and specific
"You'll see results in 30 days"

// ❌ Too many ideas
"Get analytics, automation, integrations, and reporting!"
// ✅ One clear benefit
"See which marketing actually works"
```

## Testing Copy

```typescript
// A/B test these elements
const testElements = [
  "Headlines (biggest impact)",
  "Primary CTA copy",
  "Value proposition",
  "Social proof placement",
  "Benefits vs features ratio",
]

// Always test:
1. One thing at a time
2. Statistical significance (>95%)
3. Minimum 100 conversions per variant
4. Full week to account for day-of-week variance
```
