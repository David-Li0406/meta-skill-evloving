# Goal Setting and Tracking

This skill guides you through creating well-formed, actionable goals and maintaining accountability throughout your journey. It employs evidence-based frameworks to help you craft goals that are specific, measurable, and aligned with your deeper values.

## Goal Clarification

Articulate what you want to achieve by answering:

- What outcome do I want?
- Why does this matter to me?
- How will I know I've succeeded?

## SMART Framework

Transform your goal into SMART format:

- **Specific**: Exactly what will I accomplish?
- **Measurable**: How will I track progress?
- **Achievable**: Is this realistic given my constraints?
- **Relevant**: Does this align with my bigger picture?
- **Time-bound**: When will I complete this?

## Milestones

Break the goal into milestones:

1. What are the major phases?
2. What does "halfway" look like?
3. What quick wins can I get early?

## First Steps

Identify immediate actions:

- What can I do in the next 24 hours?
- What's the first real task?
- What might block me and how can I prepare?

## Success Visualization

Help visualize success by considering:

- What will life look like when I achieve this?
- How will I celebrate?
- What will this enable next?

## Core Workflows

### New Goal Creation
1. **Aspiration Clarification**: Explore what you really want and why it matters.
2. **SMART Refinement**: Transform vague wishes into specific, measurable objectives.
3. **Timeline Design**: Set realistic deadlines with interim milestones.
4. **Obstacle Identification**: Anticipate challenges and plan mitigation strategies.
5. **Resource Assessment**: Identify what you need (time, money, skills, support).
6. **Accountability Setup**: Create tracking mechanisms and accountability structures.
7. **First Action**: Define the immediate next step to build momentum.

### Goal Review & Progress Check
1. **Progress Assessment**: Review what's been accomplished.
2. **Obstacle Analysis**: Identify what's blocking progress.
3. **Plan Adjustment**: Modify timeline or approach as needed.
4. **Motivation Renewal**: Reconnect with the "why" behind the goal.
5. **Next Actions**: Define clear next steps.

### Goal Breakdown
For large or complex goals:
1. **Milestone Mapping**: Break goal into 3-5 major milestones.
2. **Action Planning**: Convert milestones into actionable tasks.
3. **Dependency Mapping**: Identify what needs to happen first.
4. **Quick Wins**: Highlight early victories to build momentum.

### Goal Reset or Abandon
When goals need adjustment:
1. **Honest Assessment**: Evaluate if goal is still relevant/feasible.
2. **Pivot Options**: Explore modifications vs. abandonment.
3. **Lesson Extraction**: Capture learnings for future goals.
4. **Graceful Exit**: Help you let go without guilt when appropriate.

## Best Practices

- **Start with why**: Goals without strong motivation rarely succeed.
- **Make it visible**: Track progress where you'll see it daily.
- **Celebrate milestones**: Progress deserves recognition.
- **Review regularly**: Weekly check-ins prevent drift.
- **Stay flexible**: Adjust methods, not destination.

## Common Goal-Setting Pitfalls

- **Too Vague**: "Get in shape" vs. "Complete a 5K run in under 30 minutes by September 1st."
- **No Accountability**: Just writing it down vs. weekly progress sharing with an accountability partner.
- **All Outcome, No Process**: "Lose 20 pounds" vs. "Exercise 4x/week and track calories daily."
