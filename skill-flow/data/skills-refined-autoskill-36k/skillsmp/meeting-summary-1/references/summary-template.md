# Meeting Summary Template

Use this structure as a guide. Adapt sections based on meeting type and content. Not all sections are required for every meeting - include what's relevant.

## Template Structure

```markdown
# [Meeting Title / Client Name] - [Meeting Type]

**Client:** [Client name]
**Date:** [Meeting date]
**Attendees:** [List of attendees with roles]
**Facilitator:** [Who led the meeting]

---

## Executive Summary

[2-3 sentence overview of the meeting's purpose and key outcomes]

---

## Key Discussion Points

### [Topic 1]

[Summary of discussion, decisions made, important context]

### [Topic 2]

[Summary of discussion, decisions made, important context]

---

## Decisions Made

| Decision | Context | Owner |

| [What was decided] | [Why/background] | [Who owns it] |

---

| [Name] | [Title/Role] | [Relevant context, communication style, responsibilities] |

---

## Systems & Tools Discussed

---

## Pain Points & Challenges

- **[Challenge name]** - [Description and impact]

---
```

## Section Guidelines

### When to Include Each Section

| Section | Include When |
|---------|--------------|
| Executive Summary | Always |
| Key Discussion Points | Always |
| Decisions Made | Decisions were made in the meeting |
| Key Stakeholders | New people introduced or roles clarified |
| Systems & Tools | Technology was discussed |
| Pain Points | Problems/challenges were raised |
| Opportunities | Potential improvements identified |
| Next Steps | Always - every meeting should have actions |
| Key Dates | Deadlines or milestones mentioned |
| Communication Cadence | For kickoff/relationship-setting meetings |
| Key Insights | Memorable quotes or important observations |

### Adapting for Meeting Types

**Kickoff Meetings:**
- Include all sections
- Focus on stakeholders, systems, communication cadence
- Capture company background and context

**Status/Check-in Meetings:**
- Focus on: Discussion points, Decisions, Next Steps
- Skip: Stakeholders, Systems (unless new info)
- Keep it concise

**Discovery/Interview Meetings:**
- Focus on: Discussion points, Pain Points, Opportunities, Insights
- Capture quotes and specific examples

**Planning/Strategy Meetings:**
- Focus on: Decisions, Next Steps, Key Dates
- Include clear ownership for each action

### Writing Style

- Use active voice
- Be specific with names and dates
- Quantify when possible (e.g., "$25.4M target" not "revenue goal")
- Capture direct quotes for important statements
- Note who said what when relevant
- Avoid jargon - explain acronyms on first use
