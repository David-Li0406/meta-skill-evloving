# Template: Personal Story Post

## Best rhetoric devices
- **Anadiplosis** for narrative flow
- **Litotes** for humble understatement
- **Hyperbaton** for reflective moments

## Structure

```
[HOOK: Time marker + surprising outcome or lesson]

[THE SETUP: What I thought / what I was doing]

[THE TURNING POINT: What happened / what changed]

[THE LESSON: What I learned from this]

[THE APPLICATION: How this applies to you / the reader]

[CLOSING: Reflection or question]

#CareerLessons #YourIndustry [+ relevant tags]
```

## Example Structure

```
Three years ago, I nearly gave up on [your field].

I'd spent [time period] [doing something]. [Describe it].
[More detail about the effort].

[The person/situation] hated it.

Not because it was wrong. Because it was right. It showed
them [uncomfortable truth]. I'd [delivered/built/created] exactly
what they asked for.

But not what they needed.

That project taught me something I wish I'd learned earlier:
[the lesson in one clear sentence].

[Expand on the lesson - what it means in practice]

Now I start every [project/engagement] with one question:

"[The question that captures your lesson]"

If they can't answer, we're not ready to [start/proceed] yet.

What [type of experience] taught you the most?

#LessonsLearned #YourIndustry #CareerGrowth
```

## Variations

### "Failure" angle
```
[THE FAILURE: What went wrong]
[THE CONTEXT: Why I thought it would work]
[THE REALISATION: When I understood]
[THE LESSON: What I do differently now]
```

### "Journey" angle
```
[WHERE I WAS: Starting point]
[THE CHALLENGE: What I faced]
[THE CHANGE: What shifted]
[WHERE I AM NOW: Current state]
[THE TAKEAWAY: What this taught me]
```

### "Realisation" angle
```
[THE ASSUMPTION: What I used to believe]
[THE MOMENT: When it clicked otherwise]
[THE NEW UNDERSTANDING]
[WHY THIS MATTERS]
```

## Voice notes for personal stories

- Be genuinely vulnerable - not performatively
- Stories where you're NOT the hero often work best
- Specific details make it real (exact amounts, time periods, page numbers)
- Always end with something useful for the reader
- "I was wrong" is more powerful than "I was right"
- Litotes works brilliantly here - understate your successes
- Let the story speak - don't over-explain the lesson
