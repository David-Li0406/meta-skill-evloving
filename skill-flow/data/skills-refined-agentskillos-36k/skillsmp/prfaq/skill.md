# Amazon PRFAQ Writing Skill

Use this skill when creating comprehensive Amazon-style PRFAQ documents for product or service ideas. This follows Amazon's "working backwards" methodology where you write as if the product is already launched and successful.

**Template:** See `docs/templates/prfaq-template.md` for the authoritative template structure.

## When to Use This Skill

- When formalizing a new product concept for stakeholder review
- When applying Amazon's working backwards methodology to product ideation
- When creating a complete 6-page PRFAQ for leadership review
- When translating a product idea into Amazon's specific document structure

## Document Structure Summary

The complete PRFAQ consists of:

### Press Release (1-1.5 pages)
- Compelling headline with customer benefit
- Opening paragraph with customer problem and solution
- Body paragraphs on benefits, features, differentiation
- Quotes from company leader, customer, and optionally partner
- Availability and getting started information

### FAQ (4.5-5 pages with 20-25 questions)
1. **Customer Questions** (5-7) - Target audience, problems solved, benefits
2. **Product Questions** (4-6) - How it works, features, integrations, scale
3. **Business Model Questions** (3-4) - Revenue, pricing, unit economics, market size
4. **Competition Questions** (3-4) - Competitors, differentiation, moat
5. **Go-to-Market Questions** (2-3) - Customer acquisition, launch strategy
6. **Roadmap Questions** (2-3) - Long-term vision, future features

## Quality Standards

- **Total length**: 2500-3000 words (approximately 6 pages)
- **Tense**: Present tense throughout - write as if it exists today
- **Voice**: Customer-obsessed, clear, jargon-free
- **Details**: Specific metrics, concrete examples
- **FAQ Answers**: Substantive (2-4 sentences minimum each)

## Pre-Writing Checklist

Before writing the PRFAQ, ensure you have:

- [ ] Clear understanding of the target customer
- [ ] Specific customer problems to solve
- [ ] Product concept with key capabilities
- [ ] Differentiation from existing solutions
- [ ] Pricing strategy or model ideas
- [ ] Vision for future evolution

## Output Location

PRFAQs should be saved to:
```
docs/prfaq/[product-name]-prfaq.md
```
