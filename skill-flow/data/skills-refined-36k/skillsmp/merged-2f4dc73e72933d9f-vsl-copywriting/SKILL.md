# VSL Copywriting Skill

## Overview

This skill assists in creating Video Sales Letters (VSLs) by following a structured 15-chapter framework designed to enhance viewer engagement and conversion rates. It combines psychological triggers and emotional arcs to effectively communicate the sales message.

## When to Use

```
「VSLの台本を作成」
「ビデオセールスレターのスクリプトを作って」
「セールス動画の台本を考えて」
```

## 15-Chapter Structure

1. **Chapter 1: Attention Grabber & Warning** - Capture the viewer's attention instantly.
2. **Chapter 2: Real "Before" Story** - Create empathy through past struggles.
3. **Chapter 3: Initial Proof Presentation** - Start building credibility.
4. **Chapter 4: Negative Appeal - First Wave** - Highlight societal changes and future anxieties.
5. **Chapter 5: Customer Case Studies A, B, C** - Show before-and-after scenarios.
6. **Chapter 6: Positive Appeal - First Wave** - Paint a hopeful future.
7. **Chapter 7: Deeper Negative Appeal - Second Wave** - Discuss risks and past deceptions.
8. **Chapter 8: Addressing Common Objections** - Preemptively tackle 10 common objections.
9. **Chapter 9: Additional Customer Case Studies D, E, F** - Showcase diverse success stories.
10. **Chapter 10: Value Education** - Challenge common beliefs and introduce new frameworks.
11. **Chapter 11: Midway Proof Presentation** - Stir emotions with compelling evidence.
12. **Chapter 12: Final Proof - Interview Format** - Present the most persuasive testimonials.
13. **Chapter 13: Offer Presentation** - Detail the product, pricing, and bonuses.
14. **Chapter 14: Guarantee, Urgency, and Final Objections** - Address last-minute concerns.
15. **Chapter 15: Closing Summary** - Encourage immediate action.

## Emotional Arc Design

The emotional journey throughout the VSL should follow this pattern:
```
Negative → Hope → Deepening Negativity → Solution → Offer → Urgency
```

## Guidelines

- Ensure the script follows the emotional ups and downs effectively.
- Present evidence in three stages (Proof #1, #2, #3).
- Include diverse customer case studies to enhance relatability.
- Balance negative and positive messaging to maintain viewer interest.
- Create a sense of urgency in the closing to prompt immediate action.
