# Domain-Driven Design (DDD) Patterns

This skill provides guidelines and best practices for implementing Domain-Driven Design (DDD) patterns effectively. 

1. **Understand DDD Principles**: Familiarize yourself with the core concepts of DDD, including entities, value objects, aggregates, and bounded contexts.
2. **Identify Domains**: Analyze your application to identify the different domains and subdomains.
3. **Define Bounded Contexts**: Clearly define the boundaries of each context to avoid ambiguity and ensure clear communication.
4. **Use Ubiquitous Language**: Establish a common language between developers and stakeholders to improve collaboration and understanding.
5. **Implement Patterns**: Apply DDD patterns such as repositories, services, and domain events as appropriate to your context.
6. **Iterate and Refine**: Continuously review and refine your implementation as your understanding of the domain evolves.

For detailed documentation, refer to the guidelines provided in the relevant resources.
