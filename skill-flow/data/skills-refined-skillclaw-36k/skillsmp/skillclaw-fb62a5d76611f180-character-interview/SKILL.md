# Character Interview Skill

## Invocation Triggers
Apply this skill when:
- Creating new characters for a screenplay
- Deepening understanding of existing characters
- Building character backstory and motivation
- Developing character voice and mannerisms

## Interview Philosophy

Grab a couple of drinks, maybe make dinner, and get ready to interview the character you are about to create for your screenplay. These questions will help you get to know your characters on a one-to-one basis, even if the exact information never shows up in your script. As the God of your world that you created in your script, these questions will help you build a foundation and develop the characters you've created.

**Tip**: Put the questions and answers on index cards or divide each character separately. This will help create a backstory for each character later.

## The 80 Character Interview Questions

### Background (Questions 1-10)
1. What year were they born in and where?
2. What is their favorite color?
3. What type of birth did they have?
4. What makes them laugh?
5. What makes them sad?
6. What makes them angry?
7. What makes them cry?
8. How do they make a living?
9. How do they feel about their mother?
10. How do they feel about their father?

### Preferences (Questions 11-20)
11. What sport(s) do they love to play?
12. What sport(s) do they love to watch?
13. What is their favorite movie?
14. What is their favorite music?
15. What is their favorite TV show?
16. What is their favorite beverage?
17. What is their favorite food?
18. What is their political affiliation?
19. Do they gamble?
20. Do they have kids?

### Relationships (Questions 21-30)
21. Do they like kids?
22. Are they married?
23. Have they ever been married?
24. Do they believe in marriage?
25. What religion are they?
26. What is the most exciting thing that happened to them?
27. What is the most traumatic thing that happened to them?
28. What type of house or apartment do they live in?
29. How is their house or apartment furnished?
30. Do they have any physical diseases?

### Psychology (Questions 31-40)
31. What are their biggest fears?
32. What are their dreams and aspirations?
33. How do they cope with stress?
34. What is their greatest regret?
35. What do they value most in life?
36. How do they see themselves?
37. How do others perceive them?
38. What is their biggest secret?
39. What are their coping mechanisms?
40. How do they handle conflict?

### Habits (Questions 41-50)
41. What is their daily routine?
42. Do they have any bad habits?
43. What is their favorite way to relax?
44. How do they spend their weekends?
45. What is their exercise routine?
46. Do they have any hobbies?
47. What is their favorite way to spend time with friends?
48. How do they handle their finances?
49. What is their approach to work-life balance?
50. What is their sleep schedule like?

### Motivations (Questions 51-80)
51. What drives them to succeed?
52. What are their short-term goals?
53. What are their long-term goals?
54. What would they sacrifice for their goals?
55. Who do they look up to?
56. What is their biggest source of inspiration?
57. How do they define success?
58. What do they fear losing the most?
59. What would they do if they won the lottery?
60. How do they respond to failure?
61. What is their idea of happiness?
62. What do they want to be remembered for?
63. How do they feel about change?
64. What is their biggest challenge?
65. How do they handle disappointment?
66. What motivates them to get out of bed in the morning?
67. What is their relationship with money?
68. How do they prioritize their time?
69. What is their approach to personal growth?
70. How do they celebrate achievements?
71. What is their philosophy on life?
72. How do they deal with peer pressure?
73. What is their stance on social issues?
74. How do they contribute to their community?
75. What legacy do they want to leave behind?
76. How do they handle criticism?
77. What is their approach to learning?
78. How do they stay motivated?
79. What is their relationship with technology?
80. How do they envision their future?
