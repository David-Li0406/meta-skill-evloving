# Skill body

## Overview

Scientific presentations are essential for communicating research and engaging with academic and professional audiences. This skill provides guidance for creating impactful scientific presentations, focusing on structure, content development, visual design, and delivery preparation.

**Key Focus**: Oral presentations for conferences, seminars, defenses, and professional talks.

**Design Philosophy**: Scientific presentations should be visually engaging and research-backed. Avoid text-heavy slides. Effective presentations combine:
- **Compelling visuals**: Use high-quality figures, images, and diagrams instead of just bullet points.
- **Research context**: Include proper citations to establish credibility.
- **Minimal text**: Use bullet points as prompts; provide explanations verbally.
- **Professional design**: Employ modern color schemes, strong visual hierarchy, and generous white space.
- **Story-driven**: Ensure a clear narrative arc rather than just presenting data.

**Remember**: Boring presentations lead to forgotten science. Make your slides visually memorable while maintaining scientific rigor through proper citations.

## When to Use This Skill

This skill should be used when:
- Preparing conference presentations (5-20 minutes)
- Developing academic seminars (45-60 minutes)
- Creating thesis or dissertation defense presentations
- Designing grant pitch presentations
- Preparing journal club presentations
- Giving research talks at institutions or companies
- Teaching or tutorial presentations on scientific topics
