# Skill body

This skill provides a structured approach to conducting depth interviews, allowing you to extract meaningful insights from participants about their values, motivations, and needs.

## Overview

This skill includes the following functionalities:

- **Exploration of Values**: Utilize techniques like 5 Whys and laddering to discover essential values and motivations.
- **Thorough Documentation**: Record all conversations in an interview log file for future reference.
- **Insight Extraction**: Identify key insights, user needs, and potential opportunities.
- **Structured Process**: Follow a clear flow from introduction to exploration and deep diving.

## When to Use This Skill

### Career Transition
- When considering a job change but unsure of what you want.
- When feeling dissatisfied with your current situation but unable to articulate the reasons.
- When faced with multiple options and needing to establish criteria for decision-making.

### Self-Understanding
- When wanting to clarify your strengths and values.
- When seeking to articulate what brings you fulfillment.
- When aiming to envision your future career path.

### User Research
- When conducting needs assessments for new service development.
- When identifying issues with existing products.
- When constructing user personas and understanding customer journeys.

## Interview Principles

### 1. Active Listening and Acceptance

**Do**:
- Listen to the participant without interrupting.
- Accept all values without judgment and explore deeper with "why" and "specifically what."
- Pay attention to emotions and hesitations.

**Do Not**:
- Compare with generalizations or evaluate the participant's values.
- Rush to provide advice or solutions.
- Impose your own experiences or values.

### 2. Comprehensive Documentation

Create an interview log file at the start of each session and record:
- Participant's statements (verbatim).
- Keywords related to values and motivations.
- Points where emotions were triggered.
- Statements that prompted deeper exploration.

### 3. The Answers Lie Within the Participant

**Mindset**:
- Focus on drawing out insights rather than providing answers.
- Support the participant in gaining their own realizations.
- Value the process over rushing to conclusions.

## Deep-Dive Techniques

### 5 Whys

Use this technique to delve from surface-level responses to deeper motivations by asking "why" repeatedly in a conversational manner.

**Example**:
```text
Participant: "I want to work for a company that allows remote work."
Interviewer: "What do you find appealing about remote work?"
Participant: "I like not having to commute."
Interviewer: "What does not commuting allow you to do?"
Participant: "I can use that time for myself."
Interviewer: "What do you want to do with that time?"
Participant: "I want to read and study for qualifications."
Interviewer: "Why is personal growth important to you?"
Participant: "I want to enhance my market value and have options."
```

### Laddering Technique

Move from specific desires to abstract values through a series of questions.

**Question Flow**:
1. "What do you like about that?"
2. "What happens if that is achieved?"
3. "Why is that important to you?"

**Example**:
```text
Participant: "I enjoy working in a team."
→ "What do you like about teamwork?"
→ "It generates ideas I wouldn't think of alone."
→ "How does that make you feel?"
→ "Excited, as it opens up possibilities."
→ "Why is creating possibilities important?"
```

### Specificity Questions

Turn abstract statements into concrete examples.

**Patterns**:
- "Can you share a specific instance when you felt that way?"
- "When did you last experience that?"
- "What happened during that time?"

### Comparison Questions

Explore value judgments by comparing options.

**Patterns**:
- "Why did you choose this over other methods?"
- "How does this compare to what you used before?"

## Workflow

### Interview Flow

```text
1. Preparation and Log Initialization
   - Create an interview log file.
   - Confirm the purpose and theme.
   ↓
2. Introduction (5-10 minutes)
   - Build rapport and explain the purpose.
   - Conduct an icebreaker and confirm basic information.
   ↓
3. Exploration (15-20 minutes)
   - Open-ended questions to explore past experiences and motivations.
   ...
```
