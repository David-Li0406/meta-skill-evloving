# Skill body

## Overview

This skill helps you create a Video Sales Letter (VSL) script based on the Taiyo style, which has proven to generate significant sales. The script follows a 15-chapter structure that incorporates psychological triggers and emotional arcs to enhance viewer retention and conversion rates.

## When to Use

```
「VSLの台本を作って」
「ビデオセールスレターのスクリプトを作成」
「セールス動画のスクリプトを書いて」
```

## VSL Structure

### 15-Chapter Framework

1. **Introduction: Attention Grabber & Warning** (0-3 min)
   - Big hook and warning to capture attention.
2. **Real "Before" Story** (3-10 min)
   - Share relatable past struggles to build empathy.
3. **Initial Proof Presentation** (10-15 min)
   - Start establishing credibility.
4. **Negative Appeal - First Wave** (15-20 min)
   - Address societal issues and future anxieties.
5. **Customer Stories A, B, C** (20-25 min)
   - Showcase before-and-after scenarios.
6. **Positive Appeal - First Wave** (25-30 min)
   - Present a hopeful future image.
7. **Deeper Negative Appeal - Second Wave** (30-35 min)
   - Discuss risks and past deceptions.
8. **Extensive Counterarguments** (35-40 min)
   - Preemptively address common objections.
9. **Additional Customer Stories D, E, F** (40-45 min)
   - Provide diverse testimonials.
10. **Value Education** (45-55 min)
    - Challenge common beliefs and introduce new frameworks.
11. **Midpoint Proof Presentation** (55-60 min)
    - Stir emotions with compelling evidence.
12. **Final Proof Presentation** (60-70 min)
    - Use multiple interviews to reinforce credibility.
13. **Offer Presentation** (70-80 min)
    - Clearly state the product name, details, and bonuses.
14. **Guarantee & Final Counterarguments** (80-90 min)
    - Address remaining objections and offer guarantees.
15. **Closing Summary** (90-100 min)
    - Urge immediate action to avoid missing out.

## Guidelines

- Focus on emotional highs and lows throughout the script.
- Present evidence in three stages (Proof #1, #2, #3).
- Use diverse customer stories to appeal to a broad audience.
- Create a rhythm of negative and positive appeals.
- Ensure the closing creates a sense of urgency.
