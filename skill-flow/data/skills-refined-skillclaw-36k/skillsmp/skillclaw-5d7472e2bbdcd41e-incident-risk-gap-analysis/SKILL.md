# Skill body

## Purpose

This skill helps you structure your approach to incident handling, risk assessment, and gap analysis, ensuring minimal damage, quick recovery, and prevention of recurrence.

## Incident Handling Framework

### 1. Initial Response (Impact Visualization)

- **Input**: Describe the incident situation.
- **Thinking**: Visualize the impact:
  - What is broken?
  - Who is affected?
  - How severe is the issue?
- **Output**: Document the impact in a structured format.

### 2. Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys)

- **Input**: Describe the problem.
- **Thinking**: Analyze the problem using the 5 Whys technique to uncover the root cause.
- **Output**: Create a root cause analysis table.

### 3. Recovery Strategy Selection

- **Input**: Describe the recovery situation.
- **Thinking**: Evaluate recovery strategies based on urgency and risk.
- **Output**: Document the chosen recovery strategy and steps.

### 4. Step-by-Step Recovery Procedure

- **Input**: Describe the recovery situation.
- **Output**: Outline a phased recovery plan with specific actions for each phase.

### 5. Complex Pattern Detection

- **Input**: Describe the incident situation.
- **Thinking**: Identify if multiple conditions contributed to the incident.
- **Output**: Document the complex patterns and potential contributing factors.

## Risk Assessment Framework

### 1. Premise Verification

- **Input**: Describe the proposed change or action.
- **Thinking**: Question the necessity and alternatives.
- **Output**: Provide a structured response evaluating the proposal.

### 2. Risk Identification

- **Input**: Describe the current system or process.
- **Thinking**: Identify potential risks across various dimensions (e.g., operational, security, compliance).
- **Output**: Document identified risks and their implications.

### 3. Gap Analysis

- **Input**: Describe the current state versus desired state.
- **Thinking**: Identify gaps in capabilities or performance.
- **Output**: Create a gap analysis matrix comparing current and desired states.

### 4. Root Cause of Gaps

- **Input**: Describe a specific gap.
- **Thinking**: Use the 5 Whys technique to analyze why the gap exists.
- **Output**: Document the root cause and potential solutions.

### 5. Risk Response Strategy

- **Input**: Describe the identified risk.
- **Thinking**: Evaluate response strategies (avoidance, mitigation, transfer, acceptance).
- **Output**: Document the chosen strategy and action plan.

## Conclusion

By following this structured approach, you can effectively handle incidents, assess risks, and identify gaps, leading to improved system resilience and reduced likelihood of future issues.
