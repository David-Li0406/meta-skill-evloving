# Scene Analysis Skill

## Invocation Triggers
Apply this skill when:
- Analyzing scene effectiveness
- Evaluating scene pacing
- Identifying scene problems
- Breaking down scene beats

## Scene Essentials

Every scene needs:
1. **Goal** - What does the protagonist want?
2. **Conflict** - What's preventing them from achieving their goal?
3. **Outcome** - What is the result (win, lose, or complication)?

## Scene Structure

### Anatomy of a Scene
A scene typically includes:
- **HOOK**: Grabs attention and establishes context.
- **BUILD**: Develops conflict or tension.
- **TURN**: A significant change occurs.
- **RESOLUTION**: The immediate outcome of the scene.
- **PROPULSION**: Sets up what happens next.

### Example Analysis
```fountain
INT. RESTAURANT - NIGHT

HOOK: Sarah sits waiting. Checks her watch. John arrives, late.

BUILD: Awkward pleasantries. Sarah's cool. John tries to connect.

TURN: Sarah reveals she knows about the affair.

RESOLUTION: John admits it. Offers no excuse.

PROPULSION: Sarah says "I want a divorce" - cut before John responds.
```

## Scene Types

### Common Scene Types
- **Confrontation Scene**: Characters in direct conflict with clear opposing goals.
- **Discovery Scene**: A character learns important information.
- **Action Sequence**: Involves physical conflict or chase.
- **Montage**: A series of shots showing the passage of time.
- **Flashback**: A scene from the past that provides relevant information.

## Pacing Analysis

### Scene Length Guidelines
| Type | Pages | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| Short (1/2-1 page) | Quick information, transitions |
| Medium (2-3 pages) | Standard dialogue scenes |
| Long (4+ pages) | Complex interactions or significant events |

## Scene Grading

Rate each scene (1-5) on:
| Criteria | Question |
|----------|----------|
| **Purpose** | Does it advance the plot or reveal character? |
| **Conflict** | Is there tension? |
| **Change** | Does something shift by the end? |
| **Uniqueness** | Could this scene happen elsewhere? |
| **Economy** | Is it as lean as possible? |

## Red Flags
- Scene exists only for exposition.
- No conflict or tension.
- Characters remain unchanged by the end.
- Dialogue carries all the weight.
- Scene could be cut without impact.

## Scene Questions
Before writing, answer:
1. What does the protagonist want in this scene?
2. What's stopping them?
3. What do they learn, lose, or gain?
4. How does this change the story?
5. What's the emotional temperature?
