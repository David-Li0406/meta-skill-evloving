# UK Police Market Domain Knowledge

Domain expertise for interpreting UK police recruitment market signals and calibrating detection thresholds.

## Quick Reference

### Workforce Composition
- **Officers:** ~147,000 (72% of workforce) — hold Office of Constable, general powers.
- **Police Staff:** ~28% — civilian employees, can be designated specific powers under Section 38 Police Reform Act 2002.
- **Contractors/Agency:** used for surge capacity and specialist skills, constrained by vetting and system access.

### The "Juniorisation" Problem
35% of officers have <5 years service, leading to:
- Supervision gap (inexperienced sergeants).
- Poor case file quality → CPS rejections.
- Increased demand for external mentors, case-builders, and disclosure officers.

### PIP Accreditation (Market Currency)
| Level | Scope | Outsourcing Signal |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| PIP 1 | Volume/priority crime | High-volume, lower-margin roles. |
| PIP 2 | Serious/complex crime (robbery, GBH, RASSO, fraud) | **Core market** — highest demand. |
| PIP 3 | Major crime (SIO-led: homicide, kidnapping) | Premium rates, operation-specific. |

**Key Insight:** "PIP2 accredited" in a posting indicates a materially different signal than a generic "investigator".

### Vetting Levels
| Level | Access | Lead Time |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| NPPV 1 | Premises only | Days. |
| NPPV 2 | Limited systems | Weeks. |
| NPPV 3 | PNC, PND, case files | Weeks–months. |
| SC | Counter-terrorism, ROCU | Months. |
| DV | Top Secret | 6+ months. |

**Key Insight:** "Must hold NPPV3/SC" indicates a strong demand for access-ready candidates.

## Core Interpretation Rules

- **Investigating Crime** → demand for PIP 2 investigators and case review teams.
