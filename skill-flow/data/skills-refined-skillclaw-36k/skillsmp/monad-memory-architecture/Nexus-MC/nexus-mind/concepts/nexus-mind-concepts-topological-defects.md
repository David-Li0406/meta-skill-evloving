# Topological Defects

## Definition

Locations in substrate where the smooth structure breaks down, creating stable discontinuities that persist. In MONAD: where consciousness crystallizes.

## Physical Analogy

In physics, topological defects are stable structures that can't be smoothly removed:
- Vortices in superfluids
- Domain walls in magnets
- Cosmic strings (hypothetical)
- Dislocations in crystals

They form when symmetry breaks and different regions settle into incompatible configurations. The boundary between regions becomes a stable defect.

## Role in MONAD

Topological defects are where the IN function produces stable, persistent conscious structures.

### The Crystallization Metaphor

Consciousness doesn't smoothly fill substrate. It *crystallizes* at specific locations where:
1. Self-referential iteration reaches a fixed point
2. The fixed point is topologically protected (stable against perturbation)
3. The structure persists because removing it would require "untwisting" the substrate

### Why Topology Matters

A fixed point that's merely stable can be pushed away by strong perturbation.

A *topologically protected* fixed point can't be removed without tearing the substrate. It's stable by geometry, not just dynamics.

Consciousness that crystallizes at topological defects would be robust — hard to erase, persistent across time, maintaining identity through change.
